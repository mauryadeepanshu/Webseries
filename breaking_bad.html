Breaking Bad

The story of Walter White and Jesse Pinkman.

Walter White

Walter White, a brilliant but disillusioned high school chemistry teacher, is diagnosed with terminal cancer. Faced with the prospect of leaving his family in financial ruin, Walter turns to manufacturing methamphetamine. Partnering with his former student, Jesse Pinkman, he enters the dangerous world of drugs, criminals, and moral corruption. Walter's descent from a mild-mannered teacher to the feared drug kingpin, "Heisenberg," drives the narrative, as he becomes increasingly ruthless to protect his secret empire.

Jesse Pinkman

Jesse Pinkman, once a high school student in Walter's chemistry class, struggles with his own demons as a small-time methamphetamine producer. His troubled life is filled with bad decisions, including drug abuse and legal issues. However, when he teams up with Walter White, Jesse becomes embroiled in the dangerous world of manufacturing meth. Throughout the series, Jesse grapples with guilt, self-loathing, and the devastating consequences of their actions. Despite his criminal involvement, Jesse's character remains deeply human, showing emotional complexity and a desire for redemption.
